My name is Amir Mohammad Tahsiri. I'm a Computer Science programmer and algorithm developer, mainly focused on developing algorithms for how intelligent systems can exhibit genuine reasoning - a field formally known as Neuro-symbolic Artificial Intelligence.

I would say what sets my path apart is the deliberate detour I took through researching human cognition. Rather than treating intelligence of AI purely as an optimization problem, I grounded my graduate research (MSc) in Cognitive Science to begin by understanding intelligence from the inside out — how humans reason, form beliefs, navigate uncertainty and building models based on it.

This interdisciplinary foundation has given me strength in both the theory and practice of intelligent systems. I have worked extensively with Algorithm Design and Computational Modelling, and more recently have focused on strengthening my hands-on programming skills through front-end development. In parallel, my background in Cognitive Science includes practical experience in Experimental Psychology and designing and running eye-tracking experiments, allowing me to build systems grounded in both theory and real-world implementation.

Exploring computationally grounded properties in formal semantics for agent reasoning
University of West of England, Jan 2022 - Jan 2024
 +

  • Analysed and compared existing semantical strucutres on formally modelling agents' knowledge representation and reasoning process to conclude a new perspective regarding the design of said structures.
    • Most research in this area agree to reach the computationally grounded threshold of semantical structures, the agents' local states must be considered alongside the system's global states. However, disagreement arises if a structure adequately captures the local states to reach this threshold.
    • This research review presents various research with different designs for their agents' local states and by comparision, concludes that computationally grounding is not threshold-based but gradational.
    • This comparision has been shown through hypotheical scenarios (i.e., swarm of self-driving cars).
    • The theoretical and practical comparision portray a new perspective towards designing semantical structures; The adequate level of computational grounding for the agents' local states must consider the their environment; certain environments may require more abstract designs.
